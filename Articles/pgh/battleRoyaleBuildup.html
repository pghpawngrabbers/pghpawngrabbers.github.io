Twitch Clips of the Week: Battle Royale Buildup!


As the Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers gear up for another week of PRO Chess League action, you may have noticed that our team has been a lot more active on Twitch this month. Missed some of the action? Here are some of the top moments from our channel and our affiliated streamers.


Shabalov Drops Nasty Mating Attack Against Guseinov:

Who saw Grandmaster Alexander Shabalov's game of the week live on the Pawngrabbers stream last week? The four-time US Champion surprised everyone when he sacrificed ...Bxc2+! to beat Montclair's top seed, Gadir Guseinov. With the tactic, Shabalov was instantly nominated for the PRO Chess League Game of the Week.

Want to watch more of the Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers this season? Make sure to check out our channel each week for our official PRO Chess League streams!

MikeySlice is Playing the ChessOpeningsExplained Repertoire!

Mikey has been taking lessons from Grandmaster Eugene Perelshteyn on his new segment, Grandmaster Teaches Grasshopper. The Michigan-based streamer has been quick to pick up the ChessOpeningsExplained.com repertoire, and was ready to play the ...Na6 variation of the King's Indian Defense, Galaga style. Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers Take On Arena Kings!

If you're following the chess.com calendar, you might be familiar with the Arena Kings Streamer's Championship Arena Kings Streamer's Championship, a weekly tournament where Streamers can flex their muscles and win prizes. This year, the Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers are bringing three streamers to the event, all of whom you can catch on the team Twitch Channel: National Master and PRO Chess League rookie Michael Chen, Candidate Master and team General Manager Isaac Steincamp, and Assistant General Manager Nathan Holzmueller.

This past Friday, Isaac got his chance to enter the Arena, streaming from the team account instead of his usual NapoleonBonaparteIV. Despite getting a slow start, he managed his way to a seven game win streak and his 12th place finish was high enough to claim the U2200 prize!
