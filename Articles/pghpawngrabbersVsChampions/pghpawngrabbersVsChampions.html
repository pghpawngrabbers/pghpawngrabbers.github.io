Pawngrabbers vs. Champions

Recap of Pawngrabbers vs. Champions, Feb 12, 2019

The Pawngrabbers are on a roll as they achieve another decisive victory in the PRO Chess League. The match against the Miami Champions started off very promising as the first round saw the Pawngrabbers a mere half point away from a perfect score. From then on, the Pawngrabbers were able to maintain the 3 point advantage and ended the match with a convincing 9.5 - 6.5 result.   

The team's excitement was palpable:

Games in! The @pghpawngrabbers won the game against the @Miami_Champions 9.5:6.5. The Pawngrabbers are on a two game winning streak! #fire #fearthefist

— Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers (@pghpawngrabbers) February 13, 2019

The excellent 3/4 point performances from GM Sergey Erenburg and FM Edward song really brought the win home as both players pulled out all the stops in order to surmount the opponents who stood between them and victory.


Match Highlights


The highlight for this match is NM Mika Brattain's absolutely wild game against GM Rui Gao. The game that these two players played during this match is unlike any other game the Pawngrabbers have played during this year's league, and the tension on the board doesn't dissipate until the game is finished.

Brattain vs. Gao

The game features its first tactic as early as move 9 and its first sacrifice on move 16. The crazy position only increases in tension as both players start sacrificing pieces and promoting queens in a frenzy of chaotic twists and turns. In the end, NM Mika Brattain scores a spectacular victory against Miami's second board. 


Next Week: Battle Royale II


With the start of next week comes the second battle royale matchup. The Pawngrabbers' first outing in the battle royal was a good way for the team to assess its strengths and weaknesses compared to other teams in the league. Since then, the team has been on a roll, winning two back to back matches both with a convincing score. Seven new teams are the Pawngrabbers' opponents next week, and it will serve as an opportunity for them to demonstrate just how much they've learned from their previous matches.
