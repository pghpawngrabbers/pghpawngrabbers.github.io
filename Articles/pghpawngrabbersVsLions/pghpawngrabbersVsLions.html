Pawngrabbers vs. Lions

Recap of Pawngrabbers vs. Lions, Feb 06, 2019

The Pittsburgh Pawngrabbers scored a crushing victory during yesterday's match against the Lions with many exciting games spread out across the event. The Pawngrabbers were able to secure their first lead in the second round before outright winning the match in the third. The momentum didn't stop there, however, as the team scored 3.5 points in the final round anyways.

The team was very excited by this result:

This is so far the most dominating win in franchise history with stellar performances across all 4 boards. Each of the Pawngrabbers scored at least 2 points including a perfect 4 / 4 from GM Evgeny Postny and a 3.5 / 4 from GM Sergey Erenburg. This huge victory has brought the Pawngrabbers up to 6th place in the standings pulling them out of the relegation zone.

Match Highlights

Every player on the Pawngrabbers had a strong showing this week, but the highlight of the match for the Pawngrabbers was GM Evgeny Postny's game against GM Sergei Movsesian in round 3. While some of Postny's games were more even, the game shown below is a perfect example of just how overwhelming his wins in the PRO Chess League can be even against other strong grandmasters. 

GM Postny ended this game with twice as many queens on the board as his opponent, scoring his third point of the match. He would go on to defeat GM Romain Edouard in the final round and score more points than any other player in the Atlantic Division this week.

Off the Board

A day before the match started, in order to spice up the competition, both teams agreed to a bet regarding the match results: 

It seems like the @pghpawngrabbers are super confident about the match this week that they’ve taken us on in a bet! What should the stakes be? We’re letting the @PROChessLeague and @chesscom fans decide! #prochess #chess

The agreed bet was that the losing team would have to read a pre-scripted minute long speech written by the winning team during next week's stream. They would then have to clip it and tweet it. Look out for that clip coming out of Twitter during next week's match.
